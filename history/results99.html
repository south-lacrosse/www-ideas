January 23
Hillcroft 5, Kenton 10
Hillcroft were 3-0 down by the end of the first quarter but they dug in and started to dominate all the phases of the game. Dogged, careful defence meant Kenton's feeding passes couldn't find their target men and it was always Hillcroft who seemed to come up with the ground ball. Only heroic efforts by Simon Savage in the Kenton goal preserved the visitors lead. A goal at the end of the 3rd improved matters a little and  Kenton were ahead by 6-4 at the start of the 4th. Kenton scored early on  and then held on for 10 minutes whilst Hillcroft laid seige to Simon Savage's goal. Desparate measures in the closing minutes left Hillcroft open to the counter attack and Kenton were able to stretch the lead to 9-4. Hillcroft scored with 45 seconds left but Kenton responded straight from the next face to take the game 10-5, a margin which flattered them.

November 28
Hitchin 11, Hillcroft 10
Hillcroft arrived with  a strong squad, and as the game got underway, with the threat of rain sweeping across the hills, it felt like a good match up.

Hillcroft struck the first blow, with the attack forcing a long slide, dumping and finishing cleanly.  Hitchin fought back with a good period of possession and backed up shots.  Both teams fought hard and were evenly matched in most respects.   The only area where one side had a significant advantage over the other was at the face where the bulk of possession was in Hitchin's favor.  At quarter time the score was 3-3, at half time it was 5-5.  It couldn't have got any closer, but Hitchin were always playing catch up.

Clearly however the Hillcroft half time team talk had an effect as they took good advantage of a couple of fast breaks, and worked well in settled attack to put  the Hitchin defence under constant pressure. Hillcroft piled on four straight goals to lead 5-9. Hitchin spirits were clearly low, but no one was going to give up.  Steadily Hitchin  got a grip on the game once more and replied with three straight goals to bring it up to 8-9 at three quarter time.

The final quarter was high tension, drama and passion.  Hitchin were penalised and before the minute could run down, Hillcroft fed, shot and led 8-10. Hitchin pulled back two goals , and then it was 10 each with about 2 minutes remaining.

Hillcroft came up with the ball from the face and moved the ball around the Hitchin cage.  Two shots went in, both with defensive sticks just diverting them.  In the dying moments Hitchin cleared, moved the ball cleanly up against a Hillcroft defence still trying to organise itself.  A shot, hit the keepers chest, a second hit his foot.  Davey Leach scooped the ball clean from the scrum of players by the crease and slotted home with 10 seconds left on the clock and the final whistle sent the Hitchin squad in to shouts of joy.

November 14

Kenton 13, Hillcroft 8

Conditions were not great for lacrosse on Kenton's dinky sloping upper pitch but at least the necessity for scuba gear did not arise (as it would have on the usual site). Kenton's usual cutting game with feeds from behind the goal is well suited to a small pitch and the home side were soon 3-0 up. Hillcroft pulled one back by the end of the first quarter and rallied strongly in the second , dominating possession and scoring 4 unanswered goals before Chase Tydings last-minute effort bought the half time score to 4-5. It stayed close in the 3rd as Kenton took a 9-8 lead. Two goals in 11 minutes gave Kenton had a 3-goal lead as the game entered its last minutes. Hillcroft had to press Kenton's ball carriers and this meant taking risks. Kenton skillfully kept possession and exploited the openings offered by Hillcroft's death or glory approach to open up a 5-goal lead by the final whistle.
Quarter scores, Kenton first: 3-1, 4-5, 9-8, 13-8
Kenton scorers: Phillips (5), M Duckworth (2), P. Duckworth, Tydings (2), Flew(3)
Hillcroft scorers, Catto(3), Jaeger, A. Pritchard, Ryder,  Watanabe, Whitehead


Hitchin 16  Bath 3

Hitchin were only able to field 9 players but as the match started they scored from the face and the hopes of the fearful were raised.  Bath quickly struck back, taking advantage of confusion on a five man defence.  From this point however the game went all in the home sides favour.

Hitchin dominated the face off and this gave them most of the possession . The five man Hitchin offense kept the ball moving and took its opportunities to shoot.  For the most part ground balls and shots over the back line were recovered. The steady pressure was working and the score racked steadily upwards. The score at the half was Hitchin 8 Bath 1.

The second half was more of the same.  As legs became more tired Bath had the chance of more attacks, but the Hitchin defence were pretty fresh and prevented any hope of a significant Bath score. The match ran out with the rain pouring and the score at 16-3.

Hitchin scorers: Jim Gibson 2G, Justin Dicair 5G, Ryan Colucci 4A, Tom Lasky 3G 4A, David Leach 2G, David Coldwell 4G 4A.

Purley 14 , Hitchin 5


 


Transportation difficulties meant that Hitchin started this game with 8 men. For nearly five minutes the Hitchin box four defence held off the Purley attack but it couldn't last.  Purley playing 10 on 8 soon started slotting the goals and by quarter time Hitchin were 5-2 down.

The second quarter saw Hitchin  switch to a 6 man zone.  Whilst this prevented Purley from scoring for nearly 10 minutes it felt very uncomfortable and was only really successful due to some tremendous stops from Adrian Anderson who had his best game of the season to date.  Purley did start to get through again and the half ended at 7-2.

Purley  won the second half by 5 goals to 3 to produce a closing result of 14-5.  Two of the Hitchin goals in the second half earned credit for Brad Cruikshank, who started winning ground balls from the face and feeding them forwards.

Kenton 14 - Hitchin 12

 Hitchin struck first through David Coldwell.  The feat was repeated rapidly before Kenton responded in typical fashion with solid drives, and crisp passing.  At the end of the first quarter Kenton lead 3-2.

The second quarter saw Kenton keeping control, creating good opportunities and taking them well.  Hitchin were not as sure and fast in attack as they had been in the first quarter, scoring twice to Kenton's three.

In the 3rd quarter, Kenton outscored Hitchin 4-3 but it was clear that Hitchin were winning some of the battles.  The visitors had more promising attacks and more time on the ball than Kenton, but it was coming to nothing, as  Kenton stuck at driving with the ball, shooting if possible, but dumping if the slides came

The final quarter saw Hitchin slump to 11-7 down before  scoring five straight goals to lead 11-12 with about six minutes to go.But still Kenton kept on coming.  Taking the lead, Kenton played keep away.  A sound tactic that allowed them run out 14-12 victors.
